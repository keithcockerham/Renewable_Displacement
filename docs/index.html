Project Overview

Objective

This project examines whether growth in renewable electricity ‚Äî specifically utility-scale solar and wind ‚Äî has displaced fossil fuel generation in the United States.

Three evidence tests were used: differenced correlations, Granger causality, and regression effect sizes.

Data Source

U.S. EIA Electricity dataset (~1.36 GB text file), containing 751k time-stamped generation series across 50 states and fuel types.

Monthly national totals were extracted for solar, wind, natural gas, petroleum liquids, and coal.

Core Results

1. Renewable Growth vs Gas Decline

Differenced time series revealed a clear, negative correlation between renewable growth and natural gas generation changes. The pattern is consistent across multiple smoothing windows.

Interpretation: Short-run increases in renewables are associated with short-run reductions in gas generation, especially during periods of rapid solar expansion.

2. Granger Causality

Renewables Granger-cause natural gas at nearly all lags (p < 0.01). Gas does not robustly Granger-cause renewables.

Interpretation: Renewable changes improve forecasts of future gas generation.

3. No Coal Displacement

The coal time series shows no consistent displacement signal. Some lags briefly reach significance but not with coherent directionality.

Interpretation: Coal‚Äôs decline is driven by structural factors (retirements, regulation), not renewable competition in this dataset.

Methodology Summary

Data Engineering

  • Parsed 751k JSON-like series from raw text
  • Converted all timestamps (monthly, quarterly, annual)
  • Extracted generation categories: SUN, WND, NG, PEL, COW
  • Created national pivot table for 2001‚Äì2025

Statistical Tests

  • First-difference transformation to remove seasonality
  • 12-lag Granger causality
  • Regression effect sizes
  • Rolling correlations (24-month window)

Interpretation Framework

  • Focus on predictive direction, not causation
  • Evaluate robustness across lags
  • Compare renew‚Üígas, renew‚Üícoal, gas‚Üírenew
